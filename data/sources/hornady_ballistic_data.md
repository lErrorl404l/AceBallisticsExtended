## Important Notes on Hornady BCs

Hornady originally published "800-yard" average BC values for ELD-X and ELD Match bullets. They now publish **Mach-based BC values** at three reference velocities: | Condition | Velocity | Use Case |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Mach 2.25** | 2,512 fps | Industry comparison standard (equivalent to ~200-yard BC). Use when comparing to other manufacturers. |
| **Mach 2.0** | 2,233 fps | Mid-range shooting where the bullet spends time in flight after slowing down. |
| **Mach 1.75** | 1,954 fps | Long-range shooting beyond 600 yards where much of bullet flight occurs after velocity loss. |

- Hornady BC values are corrected to **ICAO Standard Atmosphere** (sea level, 59°F, 29.92 inHg, 0% humidity).
- **ELD Match, ELD-X, A-Tip, and ELD-VT** bullet BCs are **measured with Doppler radar** to ranges of up to one mile.
- Older bullet lines (InterLock, SST, V-MAX) use G1 BC values measured via conventional chronograph methods at 200 yards.
- Hornady's **4DOF Ballistic Calculator** (https://www.hornady.com/4dof) uses actual drag coefficient curves per projectile rather than BC, providing more accurate trajectory predictions.
