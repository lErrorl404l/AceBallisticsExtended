## Reference Notes

### Pressure Standards Overview

| Standard | Region | Method | Unit | Notes |
|----------|--------|--------|------|-------|
| **SAAMI** | USA | Conformal piezoelectric transducer (preferred) or copper crusher | psi / CUP | MAP = Maximum Average Pressure for 10-round sample. CIP and SAAMI pressures are NOT directly convertible due to different measurement methods. |
| **CIP** | Europe (CIP member states) | Drilled-case Kistler 6215 channel transducer | bar | Pmax = Maximum Average Pressure; PK = 1.15×Pmax max statistical individual; PE = mean proof (1.25×Pmax rifle, 1.30×Pmax pistol) |
| **NATO EPVAT** | NATO | Kistler 6215 transducer ahead of case mouth | MPa | Used for 5.56mm, 7.62mm, 9mm, 12.7mm NATO. Transducer mounted ahead of case mouth — NOT interchangeable with CIP or SAAMI. |

### Conversion Factors
- 1 bar = 0.1 MPa = 14.5038 psi
- 1 MPa = 10 bar = 145.0377 psi
- 1 psi = 0.00689476 MPa

### Key Terms
- **MAP** (SAAMI): Maximum Average Pressure — the recommended max pressure level for commercial loading.
- **Pmax** (CIP): Maximum Average Pressure (Ptmax) — transducer method at specified M (mm from breech face).
- **PK** (CIP): 1.15 × Pmax — maximum statistical individual pressure (no single round should exceed).
- **PE** (CIP): Mean proof pressure — 1.25×Pmax for rifle (Tab I/II/III), 1.30×Pmax for pistol (Tab IV).
- **EPVAT Pmax** (NATO): Service pressure measured in EPVAT barrel. Proof = Pmax + 25%.

## Critical Notes for Weapon JSON Validation

1. **NATO vs SAAMI/CIP are NOT interchangeable**: NATO EPVAT pressures are measured with the transducer mounted **ahead of the case mouth**, yielding different peak values than CIP (drilled case) or SAAMI (conformal). The .50 BMG illustrates this: SAAMI ~55,000 psi, CIP 53,664 psi, NATO EPVAT 60,481 psi — all for the same physical cartridge.

2. **CIP Pmax vs SAAMI MAP**: SAAMI MAP is the maximum *average* of a 10-round sample. CIP Pmax is equivalent. Both represent the service pressure limit for commercial ammunition.

3. **CUP vs Piezo (psi) are NOT convertible**: The SAAMI standard explicitly states "the limiting pressure values for the different systems are not interchangeable."

4. **Proof pressures**: CIP proof (PE) is 25% over Pmax for rifle, 30% for pistol. NATO proof is 25% over EPVAT Pmax. SAAMI proof is 30-40% over MAP (defined relative to MPLM).

5. **Cartridges without SAAMI standards**: 5.45×39mm, 12.7×108mm, 9×39mm, and 7.62×54mmR have no SAAMI standards. Weapon JSON values for these should use CIP data as the primary reference.
