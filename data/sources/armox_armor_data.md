## 11. MIL-DTL-46100E — High-Hardness Armor (HHA)

Source: MIL-DTL-46100E (MR) w/Amendment 1 (2008+)
URL: https://titussteel.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/46100E_spec.pdf
Status: Active, Document Date 27-FEB-2026 (per DLA Quicksearch)
Scope: https://quicksearch.dla.mil/qsDocDetails.aspx?ident_number=25177

**Scope:** Quenched and tempered high-hardness wrought steel armor plate
- Ordered thickness: 0.098 in (2.5 mm) to 2.000 in (50.8 mm)
- For lightweight armor applications

**Hardness Requirement:**
- HBW 477 - HBW 534 (surface hardness, 10 mm carbide ball, 3000 kg load)
- Brinell impression diameters on any individual plate shall not vary by more than 0.15 mm

**Ballistic Test Projectiles by Thickness:**

**V50 Definition (per Appendix A):**
- Average of 6 fair impact velocities (3 lowest CP + 3 highest PP)

## 14. Notes on Specific Grades Requested

### "ARMOX 500" vs "ARMOX 500T"
SSAB's current product line uses the "T" designation (500T). The "500" without T likely refers to the same grade. All published mechanical data above applies.

### "ARMOX 600S" (Requested)
This is an **older designation** found in 1990s-2000s literature (TNO papers, workshop recommendations). The current equivalent is **Armox 600T** (T = toughness/tempered). The "S" may have designated a different production variant or surface finish. Limited data available:
- Hardness: ~600 HB
- Density: 7.84 g/cm³
- Before the current "T" series naming convention

### "ARMOX 700T" (Requested)
**Does not exist in SSAB's current product catalog** (as of July 2026). The closest grades are:
- **Armox 620T:** 55-60 HRC (~600-650 HBW equivalent), thickness 5-15 mm
- **Armox Advance:** 58-63 HRC (~650+ HBW equivalent), thickness 4.0-7.9 mm

The highest hardness available is Armox Advance at 58-63 HRC. No SSAB product is designated "700T" or guarantees 700 HBW.
